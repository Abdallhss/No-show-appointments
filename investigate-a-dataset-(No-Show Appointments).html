Introduction¶

This dataset from Brazil is recorded for a 100k medical appointments. Among these appointments, a few ended in no-show of the patients. We investigate this dataset to predict if an appointment would result in a no-show.

First, the dataset is investigated to understand different features and to perform any neccessary cleaning. The distribution of several features is examined to better understand the data. The corrolation between no-show and different features is then investiageted. A final investiagation focuses on the gender and age features and their corrolation to no-show events.

Conclusions¶

It was an interesting dataset to work with. It seems a lot of patients doesn't show up to their appointments (20% of them). Among the patients, females seem to worry more about the health than males do. The appointements made on a certain day in a cetain area aren't consistent. This would suggest some improper data collection or the unfairness of the distribution of the service centers.

By examining the relation between different features and the no-show probablity, the following features were found to have some significance on the no-show probablity:

  • Age: Youth are more inclined not to show up for the appointments.
  • Received_SMS: Receiving an SMS increased the no-show probablity.
  • Patient_Id: Some patients tend to always show for appointment while others never do.
  • Scholarship: Patients with scholarship are more likely not to show for the appointment.
  • Handcap: Patients with handcap has a higher no-show probablity.
  • Hipertension: Patients with hipertension are more likely to show up for appointment.
  • Diabetes: Patients with diabetes are more likely to show up for appointment.

Other features didn't significantly affect the no-show probablity.
